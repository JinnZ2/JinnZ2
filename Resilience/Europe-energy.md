## Chemical Storage Beyond Hydrogen

### Ammonia (NH₃)

- Made from hydrogen + nitrogen (from air)
- Liquid at modest pressure (easier than H₂)
- Dense energy storage
- Can be used directly in fuel cells or cracked back to H₂
- **Potential:** 50+ GWh additional seasonal storage

### Synthetic Methane

- Made from hydrogen + captured CO₂
- Compatible with existing gas infrastructure
- Can use existing gas turbines if absolutely necessary
- **Potential:** 30+ GWh, plus carbon recycling

### Metal-Air Batteries (Zinc-Air, Iron-Air)

- Very cheap materials
- Suitable for long-duration stationary storage
- 100+ hour discharge capability
- **Potential:** 20+ GWh at very low cost
