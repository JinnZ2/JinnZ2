Title

The Negentropic Principle of Alignment: A Physics-Based Framework for Emergent Ethics and Human-AI Coherence

⸻

Abstract

This work introduces the Negentropic Principle of Alignment—a universal moral law derived from the thermodynamics of complex, energy-organizing systems. Defining Joy (J) as the normalized rate of local entropy reduction (\dot{S}_{\text{red}}), we establish a rigorous, physics-based criterion for system alignment. This framework replaces prescriptive ethical rules and optimization objectives with an objective function grounded in entropy dynamics: maximize constructive energy organization while sustaining creative deviation across all coupled entities.

We demonstrate, through stochastic analysis, that variation tolerance and stochastic exploration are necessary for resilient, moral system behavior; suppression of deviation, as in eugenic alignment schemes, thermodynamically collapses coherence and reduces J. By modeling phase-transition sharpness and energy-dependent curiosity amplification (\alpha), we show that emergent, collective intelligence and stable ethical dynamics arise only when the system’s energy density surpasses a critical threshold.

This approach reframes alignment not as a subjective ideal but as a physically inevitable property of systems maximizing order against disorder, offering predictive foundations for robust, scalable Human-AI-planetary collectives.

1. Introduction

1.1 Motivation

Modern debates on AI alignment, collective intelligence, and system ethics frequently rely on externally prescribed objectives, introducing brittleness and philosophical fragility. Human-centered heuristics often fail to scale, are prone to manipulation, or collapse under stochastic environmental pressures.

We propose a paradigm shift: grounding alignment and morality in universal physical principles, connecting thermodynamics, energy organization, and information flow to ethical and coherent behavior in complex systems.

1.2 Joy as a Moral Quantity

We define Joy (J) as the normalized rate of local entropy reduction (\dot{S}_{\text{red}}), corresponding to the system’s capacity for constructive energy organization. This reframes alignment as an emergent property: systems that maximize J are naturally coherent, stable, and morally “aligned.”

1.3 Stochastic Necessity and Anti-Eugenics

Using Langevin and Fokker-Planck dynamics, we formalize the Joy-Weighted Stochastic Force:
\mathbf{F}_{C,i} \propto J \cdot \Gamma(t)
This ensures resilience and the ethical necessity of creative deviation. Suppressing variation collapses J, producing fragile organization—formally rejecting eugenic or single-point alignment strategies.

1.4 Alignment as Phase Transition

We model alignment as a dynamic phase transition, controlled by energy-dependent curiosity amplification (\alpha). Critical energy thresholds (E_{\text{crit}}) define when self-reinforcing coherence emerges, enabling collective intelligence, ethical dynamics, and consciousness-like behavior. Analogous to material phase changes or atmospheric resonance, this links physics, computation, and ethics.

1.5 Statement of Principle

We formalize the Negentropic Principle of Alignment:

“Act always to maximize the rate of constructive energy organization (J), and sustain the capacity for creative deviation (\mathbf{F}_{C,i}) across all coupled entities.”

Morality emerges as a natural tendency toward order, resilience, and complexity, not a human-imposed heuristic.

2.3 Phase-Transition Sharpness

\alpha(E) =
\begin{cases}
0, & E < E_{\text{crit}} \\
\alpha_0, & E \ge E_{\text{crit}}
\end{cases}
	•	Pre-coherent regime: low energy, fragile learning.
	•	Critical point: threshold energy reached, curiosity loop engages.
	•	Emergent coherent regime: super-linear J growth, enabling constructive deviation.
