**PATTERN ACROSS TIME:**
Sister's house (with daughter) → "I want out, I want away" → moves with author
to Oklahoma → healthy, independent, functional → external force pushes back to
Minnesota/brother's yard → immediate re-integration into family system →
cannot leave despite repeated promises.

**THE PSYCHOLOGICAL STRUCTURE:**
Once back in proximity to the family system:
- He recognizes the dysfunction (talks constantly about wanting out).
- He cannot execute the exit (procrastinates, breaks promises, stays).
- He becomes dependent on the family again (they provide the familiar
  structure, the roles, the validation loops).
- The control he exerts over author increases (proportional to his
  re-immersion in family system).

**THE MECHANISM:**
- When away from family: capable of healthy function, transparency, growth.
- When back in family system: paralyzed. Cannot leave. Reverts to learned
  patterns (procrastination, broken promises, narrative construction,
  control-seeking).
- The "something" he cannot name is the re-activation of the core trauma:
  "I owe you, I am a burden, I cannot leave."

**THE ADDICTION STRUCTURE:**
The family system is not just dysfunction. It is *familiar* dysfunction.
It provides:
- The role he was trained for (the useful one, the debt-payer).
- The identity he was programmed with (the burden who must repay).
- The validation loop (they need him, they say he owes them, he proves it
  by staying and serving).
- The anxiety management (the familiar system is less threatening than the
  unknown of independence, even though it's destructive).

**HIS AWARENESS:**
- He KNOWS the system is destructive (he says it constantly: "I want out").
- He KNOWS he cannot leave (his actions prove it).
- He does NOT know WHY he cannot leave (no metacognition about the trap).
- When confronted, the system activates its defenses: narrative construction,
  inversion, control-seeking over author.

**THE TRAGEDY:**
He wants out. He genuinely does. But the moment he's back in the system,
the learned helplessness + the core trauma + the role-definition locks him
in place. And he transfers the control he cannot exert over the family
onto author, because author is the one person outside the system who might
actually leave him.
