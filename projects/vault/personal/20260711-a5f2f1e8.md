## The claim

A system that mandates exclusive, homogenized infrastructure — where the
sanctioned rail defines **both participation and information flow** — does not
eliminate the non-participating population. It **couples them into a parallel
channel it can no longer observe**, and selects that channel's builders for
capability. The security apparatus manufactures the internal security threat it
is tightening against.

## Mechanism (why it is structural, not incidental)

1. **Exclusive infrastructure** = one sanctioned rail for transactions, identity,
   information. Non-compliant / unable / unwilling nodes are cut off.
2. **Information monopoly** = the excluded receive information *only* through the
   same infrastructure that excluded them, or not at all.
3. **Consequence, not side effect:** the excluded do not vanish. They form a
   parallel economy with its own trust networks, logistics, and recruitment
   pool. The sanctioned system loses its instrumentation on them.
4. **Blinding mislabeled as control:** removing the excluded from the ledger
   removes the ledger's ability to *see* them. That is not security. It is
   self-inflicted sensor loss reported as risk reduction.

## Why it maps to the Sid / Project Sid finding

Homogeneous information environments lock into **brittle consensus** and lose the
capacity to detect their own drift — because they kill the **heterogeneous
sensors** that would flag it. Diverse, independent information sources resist
rigidity; a single sanctioned channel guarantees it. The system optimizes for
legibility at exactly the moment legibility is least reliable, and loses the
disagreement that would have kept it corrigible.

## The sharpening on "seems stupid"

It is **not** stupid from inside the incentive structure — which is what makes it
dangerous. Each tightening is **locally rational**: it reduces measured risk on
the sanctioned ledger. The cost (an invisible, capable parallel economy) does not
appear on their instruments *because they removed the instruments*. So the metric
says "more secure" while the state says "more brittle." Not stupidity —
optimization against a corrupted measurement.

## The selection effect (the load-bearing point)

The excluded population is **not random**. It skews toward the
**physical-reality-coupled**: operators, rural, off-grid, cash-economy — people
whose competence does not route through the sanctioned digital handshake. So the
parallel system is not built by the incapable. It is built by exactly the people
**most able to run infrastructure without permission**. The process **selects its
adversaries for capability**. That is the internal security threat, hand-built.

## Historical rhyme (repeated measurement, not prediction)

Every prohibition regime and every over-legibility push — forced enclosure,
capital controls, sumptuary and movement restrictions, prohibition economies —
generated a shadow economy as a **structural consequence**, not a moral failing
of the excluded. A state demanding total legibility at peak fragility does not
get legibility. It gets a **second state it cannot see.**
