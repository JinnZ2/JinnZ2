**PATTERN 8 — CORRECTED STATEMENT:**

Author paid:
- All household bills during the marriage.
- Down payment on current residence (travel trailer, titled in his name).
- Ongoing payments on the trailer (titled in his name).

**THE EXPLICIT AGREEMENT:**
This was the stated arrangement — author covers all expenses so he can
accumulate funds in his personal account. That account and its balance exist
because of this arrangement.

**THE LEGAL POSITION:**

If he now claims the accumulated money in that account is solely his, then
author is entitled to reimbursement for every dollar paid toward assets
titled in his name during the marriage.

The two positions are mutually exclusive:
- He keeps the accumulated savings as solely his → author is owed reimbursement
  for payments made toward his-titled assets under the explicit agreement.
- The arrangement is honored → the savings are the shared accumulation the
  agreement produced, and are subject to equitable division.

He cannot claim both: that the savings are solely his AND that the payments
author made toward his assets during that same period carry no claim.

**FOR COUNSEL:**
- Document every payment made by author toward assets titled in his name:
  trailer down payment, trailer payments, household bills during marriage.
- Total dollar amount + dates = the reimbursement floor if he asserts sole
  ownership of the accumulated savings.
- The explicit agreement (she pays bills; he accumulates) is the contract.
  His claim to sole ownership of the accumulation triggers her claim to
  reimbursement of the contributions that made it possible.
