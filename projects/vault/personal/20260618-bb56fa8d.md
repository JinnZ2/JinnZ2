**Analysis (factual, for counsel):**
This is a false dilemma constructed as a logical trap:
- Premise A: Author disclosed X (about a third party) to him.
- False inference: Therefore author breaks all NDAs.
- Conclusion demanded: Therefore author must break this NDA too.

The argument is structurally invalid. Disclosing within a relationship ≠
breaking a professional third-party NDA. The two obligations are distinct
in scope, party, and legal standing. The tactic uses a true premise (A disclosure
occurred) to manufacture permission to demand a breach of a separate, unrelated
obligation.

Notable: the tactic appears designed to make NDA compliance itself look like
evidence of untrustworthiness — inverting the meaning of keeping a confidence.
