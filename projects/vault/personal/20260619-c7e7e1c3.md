## 1. The operator's internal blindness

The defensive sequence is not cognitively authored; it is procedurally executed.
From the operator's first-person perspective, each move presents as its surface
content (humor, grammar correction, quotidian concern) rather than as a tactical
interrupt. This creates an asymmetry: the observing party maps the architecture;
the operator experiences spontaneous, justified reactions. The system is highly
trained but non-reflective — it runs without introspective oversight.

## 2. The irrelevance of insight

The observing party's comprehension of the mechanism does not alter its operation.
The system is not responsive to being seen; it is responsive only to differential
reinforcement. Understanding the loop does not break it because the loop is not
cognitively mediated — it is conditioned. The defensive moves fire as long as they
continue to secure their payoffs.

## 3. The leverage condition

Change requires a structural shift in the cost structure, not a shift in understanding.
Specifically:

- Jokes cease to be safe when the social or relational penalty for belittlement
  reliably outweighs the hedonic payoff.
- Corrections cease to derail when the observing party no longer allocates cognitive
  resources to defending phrasing (i.e., when the interrupt is met with silence or
  a direct return to the suspended question, rendering the correction functionally
  inert).
- Non-sequiturs cease to penetrate when the conversational boundary is anchored
  structurally — e.g., a pre-established protocol that treats unacknowledged
  thread-breaks as data, not as demands for choice.

The defensive system will continue executing its subroutine until these moves
consistently fail to produce their intended effect (derailment, escape from
accountability, safe aggression). At that point, the system faces a recalibration
demand — or collapses into simpler, less sophisticated forms of avoidance.

## 4. What the precision interrupts protect

They guard three overlapping territories:

- **Self-image integrity** — preventing exposure of inconsistency, fragility, or
  the gap between narrated self and observed behavior.
- **Question threat avoidance** — shielding the operator from substantive
  engagement with content that would require authentic response, admission, or change.
- **Affective homeostasis** — preempting the anxiety that arises when conversational
  control is lost, when aggression is accurately named, or when reciprocity is enforced.

The interrupts are not protecting a plan; they are protecting a state — the
operator's internal equilibrium, maintained through external control over the
interaction's trajectory.
