Three complementary defensive layers forming a tiered evasion system.

---

## Layer 1 — Probe-as-humor

A low-stakes boundary test wrapped in plausible deniability.

- If unnoticed: the probe lands without accountability (the target processes
  literally, missing the hostile subtext; the actor gets a free read on tolerance).
- If noticed and named: the response inverts to "you don't get humor" — recasting
  the target's accurate perception as a deficit, and the actor's aggression as
  innocent play.

Safe aggression: the payoff (belittlement) is secured either way, while the cost
(being seen as aggressive) is systematically avoided.

---

## Layer 2 — Language correction as question-block

When a substantive question threatens the actor's narrative or self-image, they
interrupt the question's momentum by correcting how it was asked.

Three simultaneous sub-functions:
- **Derailment** — the target now defends phrasing rather than awaiting an answer.
- **Authority positioning** — the actor assumes the role of semantic gatekeeper,
  defining what counts as legitimate inquiry.
- **Temporal buffer** — while the target is sidetracked, the actor's internal
  affect-driven system scours for a usable narrative response.

---

## Layer 3 — Non-sequitur thread-break

If language correction fails to fully derail, the actor introduces a complete
non-sequitur (e.g., "did you eat?").

Forces a binary choice on the target:
- **Engage the deflection** — abandons the original question and rewards the break.
- **Hold the boundary** — now appears socially awkward (ignoring a "care" question)
  and gives the actor grounds to frame the target as rigid or hostile.

---

## Cumulative Architecture

These tactics share the same closed-loop logic as the distress-veto and inversion
patterns (see 20260619-20e5b142):

- Topic control is always seized via a meta-move (correcting, joking, diverting)
  that shifts the conversational ground.
- Accountability is preemptively blocked because any response to the meta-move is
  retrofitted as the target's aggression or deficiency.
- The actor's internal state (anxiety, need for control, narrative preservation)
  is never directly examined — it is instead projected outward as the target's
  problem (humorlessness, poor phrasing, insensitivity).

The overall system is self-sealing: every defensive move contains an embedded trap
that turns the target's natural reaction into evidence for the actor's preferred
story. The only way out is to refuse the frame entirely — but that refusal itself
is then recoded as the very hostility the actor claims to be defending against.
