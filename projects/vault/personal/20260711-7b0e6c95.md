- Net: even the one clear operator action (requested closure) was overwritten

## Structural read

Same pattern as the operator-kit finding on corporate liability filtering:
the institution offloads the cost of its own system failure onto the operator,
and the **record is the offloading mechanism**. "Closed by grantor: NSF" is not
a description of events — it is a **cost-assignment**, routed to the node
cheapest to blame (the customer, who cannot counter-notate the ledger).

The record was never a measurement of what happened. It is a
liability-routing artifact. Whoever controls the ledger controls the story.

## Why it matters downstream (not cosmetic)

NSF-flagged closures are reported to banking consumer-reporting agencies:
- **ChexSystems**
- **Early Warning Services (EWS)**

An NSF/customer-fault flag there can **block opening accounts elsewhere** — the
banking equivalent of a derogatory credit mark. The in-app "please note the
account" request likely does **not** propagate to these agencies; the agency
report is the record that actually follows the operator.

## Dispute hinge (the whole case in one line)

**NSF = insufficient funds. If funds were present and the bank's handshake
dropped the transaction, "NSF" is factually wrong.** That distinction is the
entire dispute. Proof required: transaction logs / app confirmations showing
**submitted-on-time and system-rejected**, not bounced-for-lack-of-funds.
