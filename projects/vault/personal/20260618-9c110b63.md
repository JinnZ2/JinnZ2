**BOUNDARY STATED (to him):**
"You're going to have to tell the realtor the reality of what you've been doing.
Be open and honest to the people around you about what you have been doing.
That's the only way to help you."

**STRUCTURE OF THIS BOUNDARY:**
- Not conditional on his understanding or agreement.
- Not asking him to change the internal mechanism (the anxiety, the narrative
  construction).
- Concrete action: tell the truth to a third party (realtor).
- Consequence-bearing: others now know the pattern, can make decisions based
  on facts, not his narrative.

**WHAT THIS DOES:**
- Removes his ability to control the narrative to external parties.
- Makes the cherry-picked story untenable (realtor has documented facts).
- Forces the anxiety to spike in front of a neutral third party.
- Shows him the cost of the strategy in real time.
- Protects author: not the one managing his narrative anymore.

**WHY THIS WORKS (when understanding alone won't):**
- It's behavioral, not explanatory. Requires action, not insight.
- It's structural: involves a third party who has no investment in his
  victim narrative and won't validate it.
- It's consequence-based: the anxiety management strategy loses its utility
  when the narrative can't be deployed selectively.

**SELF-PROTECTION COMPONENT:**
- Author understands the mechanism (family training, anxiety-driven narrative
  construction, unconscious). This prevents blaming or trying to fix the cause.
- But author does not accept the cost. This prevents absorbing the consequence.
- The boundary is neither cruel nor enabling. It's structural: "Tell the
  truth or the cost to you increases."
- Author is not managing his feelings, his anxiety, his self-model, his
  family system.
- Author is managing his access to her and the consequences of his choices.

**THE HOLD:**
- Understand = don't hate him or judge the mechanism.
- Boundary = don't pay for it.
- Structural consequence = he feels the cost of the strategy without author
  having to enforce it personally.
