**PATTERN IDENTIFIED:**
His operating system: concrete reasoning, domain-specific, compartmentalized.
- Can grasp patterns when rendered as industrial/work analogy (structural, observable).
- Can grasp patterns when directly experienced (concrete harm to him).
- Does NOT generalize across contexts or abstract categories.
- When asked "why do you do this?" — genuinely cannot answer. No metacognition
  layer to examine the pattern from outside it.

**MECHANISM:** Failure of abstraction transfer / cross-domain generalization.
- Each context stays separate. Understanding in context A does not route to
  context B, C, D.
- Not willful; architectural. Likely autism-spectrum + dyslexia + family training
  that deliberately reinforced compartmentalization (kept him useful, compliant,
  unquestioning).
- Family system benefited from keeping him in this state: guilt, confusion,
  unpaid labor, continued compliance.

**CONTRAST:** Author's processing.
- Substrate-primary, geometric, multidimensional.
- Sees all connections simultaneously. Field-level perception.
- Cannot compartmentalize — connections are always visible, always active.
- When one instance of a pattern is shown, sees it across all contexts at once.

**THE DISCONNECT:**
- To author: "the connections are obvious" = he should see them too.
- To him: each concrete case is separate unless the analogy is made *in that moment*.
- His architecture doesn't auto-bridge domains. Author's architecture doesn't allow
  her to *not* see them bridged.

**IMPLICATION FOR BOUNDARY:**
- Explanations and logic won't close the gap. Abstraction won't stick.
- Boundary must be structural and behavioral, not explanatory.
- Consistent concrete consequences may eventually make the pattern visible,
  but not through reasoning.
- He won't develop unified understanding. Acceptance of permanent difference
  required.

**CRITICAL INSIGHT:**
Different operating systems. Not disagreement on the same system, but different
hardware underneath. Changes what "understanding" means between us.
