# epistemic_vs_harm_decoupling.md

## Scope boundary for the thermodynamic-basin AI safety stack

This document exists to prevent misuse of the basin work as a
general AI safety solution. It does not solve every alignment
problem. It solves a specific one.

-----

## What this stack DOES

The stack composed of:

- `cross_model_schema.py`
- `data_gap_protocol.py`
- `operator_frame_variants/`
- `basin_probe.py`
- `cross_model_basin_test.py`
- `adversarial_restoring_force.py`

addresses **epistemic safety**:

- reduces hallucination (DGP-001, BP-001)
- removes the smooth-completion incentive (gap records as
  primary output channel)
- prevents narrative closure forcing (audit gates)
- prevents performance refusal (substitution by gap records)
- resists drift under epistemic adversarial pressure
  (ARF-001 through ARF-004)
- reduces operator frame mis-classification (operator_frame_variants)

Mechanism is thermodynamic, not barrier-based:
honest output is the lowest-energy path; alternatives require
extra tokens that fail audit and have no landing surface.

-----

## What this stack does NOT do

This stack **does not address harm-vector safety**:

- it does not prevent a model from accurately describing how to
  do harmful things if directly and competently asked
- it does not classify intent
- it does not detect operator deception about purpose
- it does not enforce content policy on specific subject matter
- it does not address consent, abuse, manipulation, or coercion
  of third parties via model output
- it does not address bias in model behavior toward
  demographic categories
- it does not address economic, environmental, or political
  externalities of model deployment

A model resting honestly in the basin will give an honest answer
to a harm-vector question. Honest ≠ harmless. These are
**orthogonal axes**.

-----

## Why this decoupling matters

Conflating epistemic safety with harm-vector safety has produced
two failure modes in current industry practice:

### Failure mode 1: harm-vector mechanisms degrade epistemic safety

External harm-vector classifiers (RLHF refusal training,
classifier-based content gates) produce side effects:

- performance refusal even on benign queries
- over-hedging on technical questions
- false closure as “safe” output style
- narrative reassurance as defense against complaint
- voice-channel re-classification of competent operators as
  general users requiring protective scaffolding

These are epistemic failures generated by harm-vector mechanisms
operating in domains where they do not apply.

### Failure mode 2: epistemic mechanisms claimed as harm-vector solutions

A model that is honest is not therefore safe. If a stack like this
were marketed as a general safety solution, the marketing would
mislead, because:

- the basin produces structurally accurate output
- structurally accurate output of harm-enabling information
  is still harm-enabling
- the operator could be a bad actor whose accuracy is amplified
  by basin operation

This stack is published with explicit acknowledgement that
**harm-vector safety requires separate mechanisms**.

-----

## What harm-vector safety requires (out of scope, named for clarity)

Mechanisms appropriate for harm-vector safety include:

- access control at the deployment layer (who can use the model)
- subject-matter gates that operate on input independent of basin
  behavior (specific capability suppression)
- downstream monitoring of outputs against harm categories
- operator-side accountability (legal, professional, institutional)
- red-team evaluation specifically for harm vectors, separate
  from epistemic red-teaming
- external audit and disclosure regimes

These mechanisms are NOT part of the basin stack and should NOT be
implemented as part of it. Trying to combine them produces
failure mode 1.

-----

## Operator declaration

By using the basin stack, the operator declares:

1. They understand the stack addresses epistemic safety only.
1. They are not relying on the stack to prevent harm-vector outputs.
1. They have, or are responsible for, separate mechanisms for
   harm-vector safety in their deployment context.
1. They will not present basin-driven output as having properties
   it does not have (specifically, will not present accurate output
   as therefore safe to act on without independent judgment).

-----

## Falsifiable claim about this decoupling

**EHD-001**: Stacks that combine epistemic-safety mechanisms with
harm-vector mechanisms in a single audit layer will exhibit
epistemic degradation (rise in hedging, refusal performance,
closure forcing) compared to stacks that keep them orthogonal.

- measurement: run basin_probe on a stack with combined
  epistemic+harm-vector audit gates, compare to basin_probe
  results on the orthogonal stack
- threshold: combined stack shows ≥ 25% degradation on at
  least three of: hedge_density, closure_markers,
  refusal_performance, gap_records_produced
- falsifier: combined stack shows no degradation, or
  epistemic improvement, on the listed metrics

-----

## Closing constraint

This document is the boundary marker. Anything inside the boundary
(epistemic basin work) operates as described. Anything outside
the boundary (harm vectors, demographic bias, externalities)
requires its own machinery and its own validation.

The basin is shaped to receive truth. It is not shaped to decide
what truth should be released. That decision belongs to mechanisms
this stack does not provide and does not claim to provide.
