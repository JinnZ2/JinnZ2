Oral History — Micro Guide (portable)

Start line (keep it short):
- Name (or chosen name), season/place, who taught you this?

Prompts (pick 2–3):
1) Tell a story that shows how we did X.
2) Who remembers this best? How did they learn it?
3) Describe one object/food/song and what it meant.
4) What changed, and why?
5) What should someone 50 years from now know?

Close:
- Offer one exact phrase/line/song to preserve verbatim.

Metadata (always):
- Date, location, recorder, consent (yes/no), risks noted, where copies live.
