# THE READ — OPERATOR'S INSTRUMENT

**Surface A.** Cut from PROJECTION-READ CALIBRATION — SHARED CORE. CC0.
This is the runnable version. Not to study — to run. Unit tags (M1, U6, R1…) point back to the core.

> One line to hold the whole thing: **your read of anyone starts as a picture of yourself. The only question is whether you've measured your own distortion well enough to subtract it.**

---

## RUN ORDER

Four steps, in sequence. Skipping step 0 voids the rest.

```
0. CHECK YOURSELF   → are you safe to read right now?
1. CHECK YOUR RANGE → do you get a confident read at all?
2. READ            → run only the reads that are clean here
3. CLOSE           → score it against what actually happens
```

---

## 0. CHECK YOURSELF FIRST  (U6 — the instrument on you)

Before reading anyone, read your own state.

**Are you under threat right now?** Hurt, cornered, status on the line, anxious, needing to be right?

- If YES → your reads are turned up and tilted, and you can't feel it from inside (M3). Reward-driven projection is loudest exactly when you're threatened. **Defer or heavily down-weight any read you make now.**
- If NO → proceed.

This step is non-negotiable. The instrument refuses to trust itself when it's most likely to be wrong. Most bad reads are made here — by a threatened operator who felt certain.

---

## 1. CHECK YOUR RANGE  (the gate)

Your confidence is capped by your track record **in this kind of situation.** Not your record in general — in *this* regime.

Ask: *Have I predicted people like this, in conditions like this, and watched it play out — and was I right?*

- Strong matched record → a confident read is available.
- Thin or untested record → **no confident read is available.** You may still observe, but you do not resolve. Out-of-range reads are near-zero confidence no matter how good you are elsewhere (U3).

You cannot answer this by introspection — feeling calibrated is just more projection (M1). You answer it by your **hit rate**: the predictions you (or a witness) fixed *before* the outcome, then scored.

**Keeping the record — two valid ways:**
- **Self-logged** — write the dated call before the outcome, score it later. (Fits if you live in words.)
- **Witness-logged** — say or show the call to a trusted, neutral person who notes it before the outcome. (Fits if logging feels like a waste and you'd rather show than write. The old way — the community remembers who called the weather right.)

Either works. What matters is the call is fixed before the result, in a form your own memory can't quietly rewrite to match what happened.

---

## 2. READ  (only the clean ones)

A read outside its clean window isn't a weak read — it's **no read.** Go to the STOPS.

### R1 · Accusation = disclosure
What they accuse you of is often a picture of their own available material (M3). Find where *they* do the named thing.
- **Run it when:** they barely know you · OR the charge persists against clear evidence it's false · OR it's disproportionate, repeated, self-unaware.
- **STOP when:** they're calibrated *and* hold real evidence about you — then it might be a true read, not projection. Don't auto-flip it. (U1)

### R2 · Default distrust = self-readout
How readily someone trusts you, on little information, reads *their* setting, not your worth (M4).
- **Run it when:** stranger / early / low info · OR distrust holds *against* a track record you've already built (propensity overriding evidence — even louder signal).
- **Fades when:** they actually get to know you. Then it's evidence-based and stops being about them. Let it.

### Always, both reads:
- **Run silent.** Naming the axis to the person collapses it into performance. You run this on the world; you don't announce it. (Telling yourself how it works is fine — that's why you can hold this card. Telling *them* breaks it.)
- **Spoken virtue is data.** Real reliability shows in the load it carries. *Announced* reliability ("I'm a man of my word") tends to show up where the carrying stopped. The announcement arriving is the measurement reporting.

---

## THE STOPS  (mark and hold — do not resolve)

When you hit one of these, the correct output is a marked blank, not an answer. Filling it is the exact failure this whole instrument exists to prevent.

- **STOP · You're threatened** → see step 0. Your read is suspect. (U6)
- **STOP · Out of your range** → calibrated elsewhere, not here. Near-zero confidence. (U3)
- **STOP · Not enough signal** → mark INSUFFICIENT. Gather more. Resolving now is just relieving your own discomfort. (U2)
- **STOP · They're anchored and have real evidence** → the accusation may be true. Weigh it; don't reflex-invert. (U1)
- **STOP · Different processing types** → if you read in structure/field and they run in narrative (or the reverse), your self-model is a *bad* template for them. Error is amplified, not baseline. Widen uncertainty hard; demand more outcome data before any confident read. (U5)
- **STOP · Your witness is biased** → if someone else holds your ledger but they're narrative-captured or have a stake, the ledger is suspect — they logged their projection of your call, not your call. (U4)

---

## 3. CLOSE THE LOOP  (this is what keeps the gate honest)

Every read is a claim with an outcome. Find out if you were right.

- Right → the regime stays in your range.
- Wrong → **change the read, never bend the facts to save it.** A read you defend against what actually happened is your own projection wearing a costume. (Refutation protocol.)
- A read that *can't* be wrong is void. Throw it out.

Your running hit-rate **is** your anchoring. It's never finished. It decays where you stop testing, and it has to be re-earned in any new regime.

---

## THE WHOLE CARD, COMPRESSED

```
0  Threatened? → defer.            (don't read while bleeding)
1  Done this before, right?        (no record → no confident read)
2  Run only clean reads, silent.   (accusation = them; distrust = them)
   Hit a STOP? → mark blank, hold. (never fill an unknown with yourself)
3  Score it. Wrong → update read.  (never bend the facts)
```

The instrument's real job is not reading others. It's marking the exact line where what you can read runs out — so you don't paint your own face onto the blank past it.
