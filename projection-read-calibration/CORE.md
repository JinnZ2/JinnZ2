## 0. WHAT THIS IS

A calibration instrument for reading other minds *without* mistaking your own projection for insight.

It does not promise accurate reads. It does the opposite of every "read people" framework that sells certainty: it marks the exact boundary past which a read is no longer earned, and forces the operator to stop there instead of filling the gap with self-projection.

The whole instrument rests on one inversion: the universal bias that distorts social perception (rendering others from your own self-model) becomes a usable instrument *only* for an operator who has measured the shape of their own distortion. Unmeasured, the same mechanism produces confident error. The entry cost (Section 3) is therefore load-bearing, not advisory. An operator who has not paid it will read this document and receive their own projection back, labeled as insight. That failure mode is the default, not the exception (M1).

## 1. FOUNDATIONAL CLAIM (the substrate fact)

When a brain models another mind, its default operation is to run its **own** self-model and assume the other is similar. The "other" is rendered out of the operator's own material, because the self-model is the only template immediately available.

This is not a moral failing or a correctable thinking error. It is the default operating mode of social cognition, observable at the neural level (M1). Every read anyone makes of anyone begins as self-projection. The only variable is whether the operator has characterized their own projection well enough to correct for it.

Everything below follows from this.

## 2. MECHANISM UNITS

Each unit: **claim · grounding · what it predicts · falsification condition.** Each is a discrete object. If any one is falsified, retire it without disturbing the others.

### M1 — Self-model is the default model of others
- **Claim:** Predicting another's mental state defaults to running the self-model, not a separate other-simulation.
- **Grounding:** Mitchell, Macrae & Banaji (2006): medial prefrontal cortex uses self-models to predict similar others. Tamir & Mitchell (2010): mPFC activity during self-projection predicts the magnitude of the consensus estimate — more activation, more projection.
- **Predicts:** People who reflexively assume "everyone is like me" show stronger self-projection signatures and read others *worse*. People who explicitly recognize their own idiosyncrasies project less.
- **Falsifies if:** Other-modeling is shown to run independent of self-model by default (it does not, per current neuroscience).

### M2 — False-consensus baseline
- **Claim:** People overestimate how widely their own attitudes, outcomes, and behaviors are shared. This is the gentle, always-on version of M1.
- **Grounding:** Ross, Greene & House (1977), original; Mullen et al. (1985), meta-analysis of 115 hypothesis tests. The effect persists independent of case information about others.
- **Predicts:** A person's default read of a stranger is a readout of the *reader's* own setting, not of the stranger.
- **Falsifies if:** Default reads of low-information targets track the target's actual traits more than the reader's own.

### M3 — Motivated projection (threat amplifier)
- **Claim:** Projection is rewarding, and intensifies when the operator's self-image is threatened. This is the defensive, turned-up version of M1.
- **Grounding:** Neural-correlates work (motivated-projection studies): reward regions (ventromedial PFC, nucleus accumbens) activate during biased consensus estimation; emotion-regulation regions (right ventrolateral PFC) inversely track the bias. Believing others share one's view raises self-esteem and the sense of being normal/correct.
- **Predicts:** Under identity threat, an operator projects harder and reads worse. The sharpest case: an accusation launched under threat is assembled from the accuser's *own* available material (the inverse of what they fear being seen as).
- **Falsifies if:** Projection magnitude is independent of threat/reward state.

### M4 — Trust-propensity is a lens, not a verdict
- **Claim:** A person's *default* trust setting (propensity, target-agnostic, trait-based) is distinct from an evidence-based judgment of a specific target (trustworthiness). On low information, the default setting drives the read.
- **Grounding:** Mayer, Davis & Schoorman (1995); Colquitt et al. (2007), meta-analysis: propensity to trust is the lens through which others' trustworthiness is judged when target information is scarce. Trustworthiness itself decomposes into ability, benevolence, integrity.
- **Predicts:** Someone who distrusts on little or no evidence is disclosing their own propensity — their own material — not reporting a finding about the target.
- **Falsifies if:** Low-information trust judgments track target trustworthiness more than reader propensity.

## 3. THE ANCHORING GATE (entry cost)

The instrument's output confidence is **bounded by the operator's demonstrated prediction accuracy in matching conditions.** No track record → no confident read available. This is the wall that makes the tool refuse to run for the unanchored.

### 3.1 What anchoring is
Anchoring = **meta-awareness**, operationalized as *knowing the shape of your own projection's error.* Not "I see clearly" — impossible, projection is the default (M1) — but "I know which way my model skews, by how much, under which conditions, and I subtract it." A surveyor correcting a known instrument bias.

### 3.2 How it is earned (the only way)
By accumulated **prediction → outcome** comparison across varied conditions:
1. Run a model projection (predict what a person will do / mean / become).
2. Reality returns the outcome.
3. The gap is the error signal.
4. Logged across many varied circumstances, the error signal teaches the operator their own model's bias structure.

High accuracy across **varied** conditions = calibrated = earned. Variety is load-bearing: accuracy in one narrow domain is overfitting, not calibration. The gate requires range.

### 3.3 Why it cannot be self-certified
"Am I anchored?" is not answered by introspection — introspection is just more projection (M1). It is answered by the **hit rate.** The gate asks for the prediction record, not the belief. This is precisely what defeats the self-blindness in M1/M2: it routes around introspection and demands outcome data. An operator who *believes* they are calibrated but has an untested or poor record fails the gate by definition.

### 3.4 Two custody modes (both first-class)
The calibration math needs only that **prediction and outcome are both fixed before the result is known.** It does not require the predictor to be the logger. Hindsight contamination (the mind quietly rewriting its past prediction to match the outcome) is defeated by fixing the prediction in a custody the predicting mind cannot edit.

- **Self-logged** — fits narrative processors. Operator writes the dated prediction, scores it against outcome. Externalization is the discipline.
- **Witness-logged** — fits substrate processors. Prediction is spoken or shown to a witness who records it *before* outcome. The operator never narrates against their own grain; the witness holds the ledger. This is the old, rigorous form: communities held the track record of who read weather/herd/ice correctly across seasons. Validation lived in social memory, not the predictor's notebook.

Both modes are valid. Neither is the "real" one. Confidence-bounding (3.1) applies identically regardless of custody.

### 3.5 Witness-mode bonus
When a witness logs a substrate operator's predictions over time, the witness is *also* testing their own model of that operator — calibrating their read of the reader. The relationship becomes a two-way calibration instrument. (This is relational and doing-based, which is why a substrate processor will actually maintain it where they would abandon a notebook.)

## 4. READ OPERATIONS

Each read: **operation · clean when · noisy/void when.** A read outside its clean conditions is not a weak read — it is *no read*, and the operator must route to the unknown-variable register (Section 5) rather than resolve it.

### R1 — Accusation-as-disclosure
- **Operation:** Treat an accusation as candidate information about the *accuser's* own available material (M3), then locate where the accuser does the named thing.
- **Clean when:** accuser has low information on the target; OR the accusation persists against established counter-evidence; OR it is disproportionate / repetitive / self-unaware.
- **Void when:** the accuser is anchored and holds real target-specific evidence — then the accusation may be an accurate read, **not** projection. Do not auto-invert. (See U1.)

### R2 — Trust-default-as-self-readout
- **Operation:** Treat a person's default trust setting toward you, on low information, as a readout of their own propensity (M4), not a verdict on you.
- **Clean when:** stranger / early / low information; OR distrust persists *against* an established track record (propensity overriding evidence is even more diagnostic).
- **Decays when:** real information accumulates — the read legitimately shifts from propensity to evidence-based, and ceases to be self-disclosure. (Confirmed: propensity predicts trust in unfamiliar dyads, not familiar ones.)

### Read hygiene (applies to all)
- The reads run on **unguarded** behavior. Naming the axis to the target collapses the signal into performance. The instrument is run silently on the world; it is not announced to the read party. (This restriction applies to the *target*, never to the *operator* understanding their own method — which is what makes a teaching document possible without breaking the tool.)
- The appearance of *spoken* virtue is itself diagnostic. Demonstrated reliability shows in load carried; asserted reliability ("I am a man of my word") tends to appear where the demonstration has stopped. The assertion arriving is the measurement reporting.

## 5. UNKNOWN-VARIABLE REGISTER

The core function. These are states where the operator must **mark and hold**, not resolve. Filling any of these cells with a confident read is the exact M1/M3 failure the instrument exists to prevent.

- **U1 — Anchored-and-accusing.** Accuser is calibrated and holds target-specific evidence. The accusation may be a true read. Do NOT auto-invert via R1. Hold; weigh the evidence on its own.
- **U2 — Ambiguous signal.** Insufficient data to distinguish projection from accurate read. Mark INSUFFICIENT. Gather data. Do not resolve to reduce discomfort (resolving-to-comfort is M3 firing).
- **U3 — Operator out of regime.** Operator is calibrated in domain A and reading domain B. Confidence → near zero regardless of A-record. Calibration does not transfer across regimes.
- **U4 — Witness uncalibrated.** Logger is narrative-captured or has stakes. Ledger is suspect — the witness logged their projection of the prediction, not the prediction. (Explains why the wrong witnesses cannot hold an accurate account of a substrate operator: they record their own projection.)
- **U5 — Cross-type read.** Operator and target run different processing types (e.g. substrate reading narrative, or narrative reading substrate). Self-model is a poor template for a different architecture, so M1 error is *amplified*, not baseline. Mark CROSS-TYPE; widen uncertainty; require more outcome data before any confident read.
- **U6 — Operator under threat (turn the instrument on yourself).** If the operator is currently in identity threat, their own reads are contaminated by motivated projection (M3) — reward-driven, turned up, and invisible from inside. Before reading anyone, the operator checks own threat state. Threatened operator → reads suspect → defer or down-weight. This cell makes the instrument refuse to trust itself when it is most likely to be wrong.

## 6. REFUTATION PROTOCOL

The instrument must be falsifiable per use, or it is a horoscope.

- Every read is a testable claim with an outcome. Score it.
- When a logged read is contradicted by behavior over time: **update the read. Never bend the evidence to protect the read.** (When data refutes a claim, change the claim, not the instrument.)
- A read that cannot be wrong is void. A read held against contradicting outcomes is the operator's own projection defended as fact — M3, the failure mode, running unchecked.
- The operator's running hit rate is the live readout of their anchoring (Section 3). It is never "done." It decays in untested regimes and must be re-earned.

---

## 7. WHAT THIS CORE DELIBERATELY DOES NOT DO

- It does not output verdicts about persons. It outputs reads with bounded confidence and marked unknowns.
- It does not certify operators. It reports operator confidence as a function of demonstrated, condition-matched accuracy.
- It does not resolve ambiguity. Where the signal runs out, it stops and marks the boundary — so the operator does not fill the unknown with self-projection, which is the default (M1) and the whole reason the instrument exists.
